## Electorincs Projects Overview
# Emil Rosenberg, Murad Shirinov, Fikrat Qurbanov, Mehemmed Memmedov

There is a quiet kind of violence in overwork — one that wears the mask of ambition. We glorify the sleepless nights, the skipped meals, the calendars with no white space, as though exhaustion were a badge of honor rather than a warning sign. But the human mind was never built to run like a machine. It was built to create, to rest, to wander, and to wonder. When we strip away the pauses, we don't produce more — we hollow ourselves out, trading depth for output, meaning for metrics. The most important work you will ever do requires a version of you that is present, rested, and whole. You cannot pour from an empty vessel, and you cannot think clearly through a fog of fatigue. Overwork doesn't make you more valuable — it makes you less of yourself. And the world doesn't need a burned-out version of you working harder. It needs the fullest version of you, working with intention.
